October again?

As I write this blog at 1 a.m. on October 1, sitting in my hostel room, I'm filled with a mix of emotions as I near the completion of almost two years at this college (we were the asynchronous batch). How did I manage to survive these two years? It’s been a rollercoaster ride, with moments of extreme joy and times when I just wanted to leave everything and go back home.

When I look back on myself, I realize how much I've changed, and how much this place and its people have taught me. I can truly say that IITK was one of the best decisions of my life, though sometimes, circumstances make me question that. The first-year Ishi, who wanted to be a perfectionist, has now realized that it's okay not to be okay. The first-year Ishi, who used to attend every lecture with full attention, now skips classes that don’t have attendance. The first-year Ishi, who thought that academics were everything, has now realized the importance of balance in life. The first-year Ishi, who used to sleep at 10 p.m. sharp, is now an expert at pulling all-nighters.

The first-year Ishi, who was content with her books and four walls, now craves to explore even a little bit of life. The first-year Ishi, who used to cry over small things, has learned to save her tears for the bigger moments. The first-year Ishi, who was scared of everything, is no longer afraid. She has learned to embrace even the smallest things. These past two years have taught me more than any book or person ever could. As people say, when you go to IIT, you’ll find the brightest minds, and I certainly did. But what I found even more were the kindest people and a few of my very best friends. Without them, none of this would have been possible.
